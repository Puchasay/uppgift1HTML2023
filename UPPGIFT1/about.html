About us

The invention of socks, as we know them today, doesn't have a single specific inventor. Socks are believed to have a long and ancient history that predates recorded history. They likely developed independently in various cultures over time.

The earliest forms of socks were simple foot coverings made from animal skins, furs, or woven fabrics. These early foot coverings were designed to provide warmth and protection for the feet, making walking more comfortable.

As civilizations advanced and textile production techniques improved, socks evolved into more sophisticated garments. Knitting, in particular, played a significant role in the development of modern socks. By the Middle Ages, knitted socks had become quite common in Europe.
