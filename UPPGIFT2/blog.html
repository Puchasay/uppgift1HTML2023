Borobudur Temple, Yogyakarta

Borobudur Temple is one of the most iconic and significant Buddhist temples in the world, and it's a UNESCO World Heritage Site.Borobudur Temple is located in Magelang, Central Java, Indonesia, which is about 40 kilometers (25 miles) northwest of Yogyakarta.

Borobudur was built in the 9th century during the Sailendra dynasty. It is a massive structure that consists of nine stacked platforms topped by a central dome. The temple was abandoned for centuries and was only rediscovered in the 19th century. It has since been restored and is a popular pilgrimage site for Buddhists.

Take your time to explore the temple. It's a labyrinth of galleries with intricate carvings and statues that depict Buddhist teachings and stories. If you want to witness a magical sunrise or sunset at Borobudur, consider arriving early in the morning or in the late afternoon. The views are spectacular, and it's a great time for photography.

In addition to Borobudur, you can explore other attractions in the area, including Prambanan Temple, Merapi Volcano, and Yogyakarta's cultural and culinary scene.

Bromo Mount, East Java

Mount Bromo is located in East Java, Indonesia, in the Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park. The nearest major city is Surabaya, which is well-connected by air and road. Visiting Mount Bromo in East Java, Indonesia, is a remarkable adventure, offering stunning volcanic landscapes and breathtaking sunrises.

Watching the sunrise from Mount Penanjakan is a must-do experience. To catch the sunrise, you'll need to start your journey in the early morning hours. Many visitors opt for a guided sunrise tour that includes transportation to the viewpoint. Be prepared for chilly temperatures at this time, so dress warmly.

Exploring the area around Mount Bromo often involves hiring a 4WD jeep. These tours take you to key viewpoints and attractions like the Sea of Sand and the viewpoint at Kingkong Hill.

You can hike to the rim of the Bromo crater to get a closer look at the volcanic activity. This hike is relatively easy but can be quite dusty, so it's advisable to wear a mask.

The best time to visit Mount Bromo is during the dry season, which typically runs from May to October. The weather is more predictable, and you have a better chance of clear views. However, it can get crowded during these months, especially on weekends.

Mummies in Tana Toraja

The mummies of Toraja, Indonesia, are a fascinating cultural phenomenon found in the Toraja region of South Sulawesi. These mummies are known locally as "Tau Tau" and "Lembus" and are part of the elaborate and unique burial customs of the Toraja people.

The mummification practices in Toraja are closely tied to their beliefs about the afterlife. Torajans believe that the deceased person's soul remains with their family until the body is properly buried. The mummification process helps preserve the body for an extended period while the family prepares for the elaborate funeral ceremony.

The mummification process in Toraja involves a series of rituals and ceremonies. It typically begins with the deceased being cleaned and dressed in their best clothes. The body is then placed in a special room, often a designated part of the family's house, where it is exposed to smoke from a fire for an extended period. This process slowly dries and preserves the body.

In some Toraja communities, wooden effigies called "Tau Tau" are created to represent the deceased. These effigies are typically made to resemble the deceased and are placed in balconies or on cliffs overlooking the village as a sign of respect and remembrance.

After the funeral ceremony, the deceased is temporarily buried in a stone tomb or a carved-out chamber in a cliff, often shared with other family members. These temporary resting places are known as "lantang" or "liang." Some Torajans opt for reburial ceremonies, where the remains of the deceased are transferred to a more permanent location.

Meet The Giant Lizard, Komodo in Labuan Bajo

Komodo Island is part of Komodo National Park, which is located near Labuan Bajo on the western tip of Flores Island in Indonesia. Labuan Bajo is the main gateway to the park and the starting point for most Komodo dragon tours.

The main attraction of Komodo Island is, of course, the Komodo dragons. These giant lizards are the largest in the world and can only be found in this region. Guided tours will take you to the best spots to observe them safely.

In addition to Komodo Island, the park is home to many other beautiful islands, each with its own unique features. Island-hopping tours are a great way to explore the pristine beaches, clear waters, and diverse marine life of the area.

Komodo National Park is renowned for its excellent diving and snorkeling opportunities. The underwater world here is teeming with colorful coral reefs and marine creatures. If you're a diver, consider bringing your gear or renting equipment in Labuan Bajo.

The best time to visit Komodo Island and Labuan Bajo is during the dry season, which typically runs from April to December. The wet season (January to March) can bring heavy rainfall and rough seas.

Snorkeling in Bunaken Island

Bunaken Island is part of Bunaken National Marine Park, located just off the coast of Manado, in North Sulawesi, Indonesia.

Visiting Bunaken Island near Manado, Indonesia, is a fantastic opportunity for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts to explore one of the world's most biodiverse marine ecosystems.

The Bunaken National Marine Park is renowned for its stunning coral reefs and diverse marine life. It's home to a wide variety of fish species, including reef sharks, turtles, barracudas, and colorful coral formations. It's a paradise for snorkelers and divers.

Whether you're a certified diver or a snorkeler, Bunaken offers incredible underwater experiences. There are dive centers and resorts on the island that can arrange guided dives and snorkeling trips. The dive sites around Bunaken cater to divers of all levels, from beginners to advanced.

Raja Ampat, most beautiful underwater view

Visiting Raja Ampat in Indonesia is a dream come true for nature enthusiasts and diving aficionados. Raja Ampat, located in the West Papua province of Indonesia, is renowned for its pristine marine biodiversity, stunning landscapes, and rich cultural heritage.

Raja Ampat is often referred to as the "Amazon of the Seas" due to its unparalleled marine biodiversity. The coral reefs here are among the most diverse in the world, teeming with colorful fish, sharks, manta rays, and other marine life.
